The Third is my best friend at the time, a boy.

Not long after I got slapped by the Second, I was no longer able to socialize with people in my school, not without them looking at me with fear and talking behind my back.

Only one boy was able to treat me normally.

We have been friends since the orientation period, at the beginning of our Junior High School era. He knows me well enough, and I believe that I know him just as well.

We often went on hangouts near a PC rental place, or an internet cafe after school, or played soccer together, or playing anything else.

Playing with him felt the same as playing with my old childhood friend, the First. He almost feels like a replacement of sorts.

After the incident, there was no change in our friendship. We still talk with each other, and we even discuss about the fight. I explained only half of the reality of the situation, the part that I'm comfortable telling.

Maybe I should've been more clear to him, or maybe that won't change a thing.

Soon after, I can feel him distancing away from me. Our usual routine of playing breaks, and one day it even stops.

We rarely even talk after a while.

One day, when I had enough of the judging eyes people were giving me, I noticed him talking with one of the cronies of that coward.

I heard the conversation. "You know him, he's not a good guy..." Just another voice in the sea of people talking about my temper. I almost got myself mad again and wanted to confront him, but I stopped myself. I promised that I wouldn't fight again.

I stood still and listened on. "Yeah, I mean... I didn't know he was like that..." Turns out he already planned to distance himself away from me after the incident. "He's too aggressive..."

Usually in stories like this, the characters would say that they feel like time stopped for them when they got betrayed like this, but not for me.

The only person I could trust, that I think could understand me...

Without thinking, I moved myself to face them. I stare straight into my friend's eyes, and say,"You don't need to be friends with me anymore if you feel like you're forced to be. Thank you for everything."

And I learned to be alone since then, learned to survive against those who judged me for what I did.

One year later, my Junior High School era ended, and those shadows I left behind as my father moved away to the Capital for work.

A bright shadow awaits me.
