The Second

The Second was my cousin, my older cousin.

I shared a school with her during my Junior High School, and yes, it's another woman. I swear, not all of my shadows are feminine in nature.

We only have a difference of one year in age, and I often say hi to her when we pass by.

But we're not that close, to begin with. I only know her after meeting her at a regular family gathering. Her father is my uncle, my mother's younger brother.

One day, something changed between us.

I was greeting her like usual, but it's not a kind face or a greeting back that I get in return. She was silent instead, and she looked worried.

The next thing I knew, I was accosted by two students, the same year as me. I know these two in particular, friends of a particularly annoying guy in my class. A famous student, not just because of his looks, but because of his wealth.

They didn't get into my face about the whole thing, though.

"Hey... so here's the thing." They began explaining. The famous guy is dating someone now.

I was confused. "What does that have to do with me?"

Then they revealed that my cousin was the one he was dating.

I was asked nicely to distance myself away from her. Very nicely.

"Where can I find this guy?" I was going to yell it out, but I kept myself calm and asked just as calmly as they were doing to me.

They answered, not knowing what I was about to do. I took off, my heart almost leaping out of my chest. In anger? I couldn't tell.

As soon as I saw his ugly face in the distance, I began to run. I heard the footsteps of his cronies behind me, following me after I just left them behind.

It was too late for them to stop me. My fist flew to his cheeks, and he ate it like it was his breakfast for the day.

That's not the only thing I gave him.

It was a full-fledged fight. Turns out, he's not all looks, he can dish some as well. We were both wounded before someone stopped the both of us from continuing.

I remember that in the middle of the fight, we were aggressively yelling at each other. I said that I will not bend my knee to his stupid ass, and he didn't reply, just defending himself and punching me in anger.

At some point, I do remember telling this coward that she's actually my family, my cousin.

We were both called to the principal's office, and we're both reprimanded, suspended, and forced to shake our hands in order to "make up".

After everything is over, and we were out of the office, my cousin approached us. She gets worried over her boyfriend's state, and then she looked at me.

I never knew that such soft hands can produce quite the slap.

The slap was loud enough that it echoes off the walls, and it was loud enough that other students stop and stare at us.

The look she gave me that day is still rattling around in my mind, even now. My cousin never forgave me, and we never spoke again with each other afterwards.

Even when later on she realized that her boyfriend was not a good guy after all, and they split up, we never made up.

Every family gathering afterwards is filled with awkwardness, and my mother stopped asking me to join them at some point.

The shadows line up, waiting for me to remember them.
