When I first saw Kisako Yama, I thought I finally was able to fulfill a lifelong dream.
Since I was a child, I already decided that I wanted to be with someone. As childish as that thought was, it started to blossom, and bloomed, into a full-blown need to have a partner.
Or at least, paired with someone that I truly love from the first sight, and they also love me at first sight.
The lady in front of me is elegant, beautiful. Straight dark hair, perfectly manicured fingers. Pursed lips, thin figure wrapped in the customary white gown worn by brides, while I stood awkwardly wearing the customary dark suit for a groom.
But that euphoria soon faded, and replace with grim reality, as I saw the glimpse of her looking at me, and I can see it as clear as the sky during daytime. She wasn't fond of me. I was sporting a messy black hair myself, looking pathetic with black bag under my eyes that the make up artist desperately trying to cover, and standing almost hunchbacked.
I sighed inwardly. Of course, it was just a childhood dream. An errant wish that was born out of a child's desire, as useless as a shooting star that nobody witnessed.
"Kisako Yama, do you take... Grant Illa, as your lawfully wedded husband?"
She shifted her eyes towards the ground, making sure not to look around and seeing the many eyes that are observing the ceremony. Or the guards with their weapons.
"I... do."
The arbitrator, as we call those who wed people now, smiled with satisfaction after waiting with bated breath when Kisako didn't reply, and turned towards me.
"And you, Grant Illa, do you take Kisako Yama as your lawfully wedded wife?"
I did not shift my eyes as I look Kisako straight in the eyes, and answered as resolutely as I can.
"I do."
"So by the power bestowed to me by the United..."
The words began to blur after that as we were both ushered away, to take off our gown and suits, then to where? I don't even know.
But, it was official. I am now married... or at least, "paired", to someone else. Someone that I didn't even pick.
Someone that the government arranged for me to marry.
